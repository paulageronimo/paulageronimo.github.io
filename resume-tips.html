Meeting Notes

  1. Importance
    1. A well-known fact is: A recruiter typically spends around 6 seconds reading your resume. Not-as-well-known fact: for most companies, resumes are first filtered through an Applicant Tracking System (known as ATS) before it even reaches a human.
    2. Within this changing world and the growth of ATS, there are hundreds of applicants per job posting
    3. Therefore we shall find ways to best optimize our resumes!
    4. With generalized resume banks, such as the ones for career fairs (SHPE convention), keywords are.. KEY!
    5. I will lead this session with a more technical perspective since at the end of the day it is how we can “HACK”, more like leverage the system that is in place :)
  2. I present, the three resume development thought levels: 
    1. General: beginner: resume basics, intermediate: leveling up, and advanced: networking. 
    2. Beginner or newbie level:
      1. Includes: layout and important sections.
      2. Have to distinguish your resume: Traditional vs. Creative
        1. It is recommended, that Unless you are going for a marketing or creative role, keep your resume functional
        2. What that means is keeping it in a very readable format
          1. Template provided in my website which I will link afterwards
      3. Continuing beginner level, Important sections for a resume:
        1. Education
        2. Experience
          1. Industry-specific terms (Finance vs. Tech vs. Medical vs. Aerospace for example)
          2. Relate the best you can to job descriptions
        3. Leadership
          1. Role in your local SHPE chapter, other school/work organization(s), volunteer work
        4. (If space available, or for People interested in CS or Data) Projects
          1. In-class or passion projects
        5. Skills section
          1. Prioritize listing hard skills
          2. List other languages you may speak in (if applicable)
    3. Intermediate:
      1. Includes: Bullet formatting (content of resume)
      2. Impact/Achievements….
        1. If they see you were a team lead for a project or even a cashier, they can already assume what you did, and who knows! Other applicants could have similarly held those roles as well, what is important is what sets YOU apart from them. What makes you unique
        2. HR people reviewing resume OR team hiring leads familiar with work. Data-oriented people want to see numbers! Action verbs!
          1. For those who don’t know, action verbs are the first word you begin each bullet with. Terms like “Managed” “Created” “Initiated”, etc.
        3. Google X-Y-Z Format: Accomplished [X] as measured by [Y], by doing [Z]. X = Task or Goal; Y = specific metric ; Z = action taken
          1. Example:
            1. Instead of “Assisted project managers with providing datasets” 
            2. Do “Achieved a 20% increase in project efficiency as measured by completion time, by implementing streamlined data analysis techniques.”
          2. No one is perfect! I do not have the perfect resume
      3. Key terms/words that align with job positions will hopefully fall into place as you do this
    4. Advanced:
      1. Includes networking!
      2. Leverage LinkedIn 
        1. See people’s profiles whose resumes are in your desired roles or industry, preferably if they are similar university alumni or other SHPE peers!
        2. Highly recommended: Hit them up for a small chat, they would be more than glad to help or provide guidance, some may even refer you to a job position or positions for you to consider
  3. General advice:
    1. For those who are underclassmen, do not shy away from including projects or experiences from high school, or early career members to take items from your earlier college years
    2. Ways to get more experience: Work a part-time job on campus, utilize your university’s career center, free online resume checkers like VMock.
